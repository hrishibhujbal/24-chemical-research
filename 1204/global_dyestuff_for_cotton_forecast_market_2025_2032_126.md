</p><p><strong>Cotton, being a natural cellulose fiber, requires specific types of dyes that are chemically compatible. These typically include reactive dyes, direct dyes, vat dyes, and sulfur dyes. Among them, reactive dyes are the most popular for their excellent fastness properties and bright shades. Dyestuffs are broadly classified into two types: natural dyes derived from plant or animal sources, and synthetic dyes manufactured using petrochemical derivatives. Synthetic dyes dominate the market due to their cost-effectiveness, color variety, and consistency.</strong></p><p>
</p><p><strong>These dyestuffs are used extensively across various end-use industries including textiles (woven and non-woven fabrics), healthcare, personal care, and home furnishings. The demand for cotton-based products and fashion trends strongly influences the dyestuff market.</strong></p><p>
